Introduction to Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT): The Process, Fees, Requirements and More

With the increased use of the internet, more online e-commerce platforms are emerging. The use of digital payment systems to make payments or transfer money is the way to go. All types of businesses, whether small or large, need to utilize electronic fund transfers to receive payments from clients.

The EFT concept is an essential aspect of every digital payment process. Merchants, entrepreneurs, and customers should understand how the EFT process works.

What is Electronic Fund Transfer?

Electronic fund transfer is the digital movement of money from one bank account to another. This procedure takes place over a computerized network. It does not involve any form of paperwork and takes place independently without the involvement of bank employees.

Electronic Funds Transfer is becoming more popular in the transfer of funds and payment procedures. Since the time businesses adopted digital payments, paper checks have become obsolete due to delays and expensive transaction costs.

The sending and receiving accounts do not need to be on the same bank for the transaction to occur. Both parties can use different devices such as computers, card processors, or phones to initiate the transfer process.

What is the difference between EFT and ACH?

Many people cannot differentiate between the two. ACH is an Automated Clearing House and consists of an interconnection of financial institutions whose primary purpose is to offer security in money transfer. In ACH, the transaction request will stop between the initiation and the bank for verification purposes. It may take one or two days, depending on the speed.

EFT is a general term for all digital transactions. Therefore, ACH is a form of electronic fund transfer. However, it is essential to note that not all EFTs are ACH transactions. Businesses and consumers have the right to decide on which EFT is suitable for their needs.

How Does Electronic Funds Transfer Work?

Many people are unaware of how the EFT process takes place. However, before adopting any digital transfer method, it is crucial to understand how it works. The information will let you know how to utilize the preferred payment system.

EFT transactions take place using a computer or phone with an internet connection. It does not involve cash since the process involves the transfer of data. The procedure is straightforward. The sender initiates the request through a series of digital networks through the internet or a payment terminal to the receiver’s bank account.

The sender can be anyone from a buyer, employer, or individual paying for a service. In most cases, recipients are employees, suppliers, service providers, or retailers.

What are the Different Types of EFT-Payments?

Several Electronic Funds Transfer payment channels exist today. Each of these methods provides an easy and fast delivery, making each popular. The most common Electronic Funds Transfer methods include:

Debit and Credit card Transactions

Card transaction is a common form of payment available in almost all countries. Individuals can use their cards to perform any transaction. The transactions can take place online or in-person where the cardholder swipes, dip or insert the card into a digital card processor.

During this action, the information is transferred electronically, which follows the approval of the payment withdrawal. The payment process might take one or two days to reflect on the receiver’s bank account.

Direct Bank Deposits

This kind of payment involves depositing cash directly into the receiver’s bank account without any paperwork. Employers mainly use this method when paying salaries to their employees. However, one needs to have the recipient’s bank account details.

Phone Money Transfer

Phone payments are the most popular type of money transfer used by millions of people worldwide. The increased use of smartphones contributes to the growth in mobile phone payments. These payments provide a quick and easy way to make payments anywhere and any time since people carry their phones everywhere.

This form of payment utilizes an individual’s line. The receiver gives details such as the mobile number, and the funds are deposited directly from the sender’s line.

Electronic Checks

The payer generates a digital check with electronic checks by authorizing the payment. This action can be done through the phone or online. After authorization of the payment, the business can set up to receive the payment. The details are entered into the payment processing system. Once completed, the request goes through a confirmation process, and funds are transferred to the seller from the buyer’s account.

The EFT-Payment Processing Period

Several factors determine the time it will take for an electronic funds transfer. Some of the main factors include:

  • The kind of payment
  • The service provider
  • The payment submission time

While some methods may take minutes to complete the transactions, some will take an average of one to four days. Most electronic fund transfers mainly happen during business hours. However, some channels such as card and phone payments will work any time of the week.

 What are the Advantages and Disadvantages of Electronic Fund Transfers?

Advantages of EFT

Provides Fast Access to Money

Fast access to money is one of the main advantages of electronic funds transfer. Business people prefer using the Electronic Funds Transfer process as it gives them the freedom to access their money at any time without the need to visit the bank.

It helps save time and reduces transaction costs. On the other hand, customers prefer making payments using this process since it is fast and secure. It is also easy to follow up on their expenses.

EFT channels are safe and secure

The online banking industry is rapidly growing due to the increased number of online businesses. Despite a few cases of fraudsters and hacking, most online payment systems have put in place strong measures to curb these vices.

Therefore electronic fund transfer is one of the safest payment methods to use. Companies such as Verv offer their clients with secure payment gateway.

It does not require the use of credit or debit cards

Several online businesses receive payments through electronic checks. Buyers do not need to provide their card details to authorize payment from the bank. The responsible bank will deposit the money directly to the receiver’s account.

Disadvantages of Electronic Fund Transfer

You may not recover your money after a wrong transaction

If you enter the incorrect details of the recipient, it may not be easy to recover the funds or trace who the recipient is. The funds may go to an overseas account, making it harder to retrieve since banks do not have any right to make reversals without authorization from the account holder.

Some EFT channels charge high transaction costs.

Although a few of these channels charge fewer transaction fees, some charge very high prices for their services. It is important to note that transactional fees for each company differ. Therefore, one should select a service provider with the cheapest services without compromising on the quality of service.
