Traditional Banking Institutions; Pros and Cons

The banking future is changing drastically. Increased interest rates, reduced transaction fees, and increased internet users have made online banks more popular than traditional banks. The high costs of traditional banking have made many people switch to online banking.

Therefore, most brick-and-mortar banks are turning to online banks to cope with technological advancements.
Traditional banks are trying to simplify their structure to help them minimize their operational costs. Currently, most common banks focus on their competencies rather than trying to excel in all aspects.

Most fintech companies such as Verv try to take advantage of traditional banking weaknesses to change the banking landscape. The banking industry is likely to change in the coming years, with little to no doubt.

What is Traditional Banking?

The banking business is one of the old-fashioned industries still operating. Therefore, traditional banking comprises banks that offer physical building services and have a domestic banking license. Some of the main features of traditional banking include:

  • They have a physical presence
  • They have their own branded debit and credit cards
  • They have several members of staff
  • They have dedicated account managers
  • They offer face-to-face customer service

Most banks try to meet each customer’s needs despite several traditional banking problems by adopting electronic banking systems while providing common banking services such as in-person assistance and loans.

Online Banking Vs Traditional Banking

Online banking is becoming very common with the widespread use of the internet. However, most people stick to traditional banking due to the relationship with common banks and difficulties in using digital payments.

Online banking allows you to make payments conveniently with fewer fees. However, you can only access online banking through your phone or computer. Financial institutions such as Verv allow clients to open a bank account and make payments online. It has a secure payment gateway that ensures that all transactions are safe.

The main features of online banks include:

  • Online banks do not have a physicals location
  • Online banks offer online customer service
  • Online banks have low or no transaction costs
  • They provide clients with high-interest rates on savings accounts
  • Clients access the services using a mobile app

What are the Pros and Cons of Traditional Banking?

Most people view regular banking systems as an old-fashioned system. However, most people stick to common banking due to the good relationship they build with their clients. Knowing some of the advantages and disadvantages can help you decide between traditional banking vs modern banking.

Advantages of Traditional Banking

They Offer Several Banking Options

One of the main features of traditional baking is that it provides a wide range of accounts. Unlike online banks, clients can choose to open different types of accounts that have varying benefits. Examples of these accounts include:

  • Business checking account
  • Trust fund account
  • Savings account
  • Checking accounts

Brick and mortar banks also offer a huge number of services. Clients with different accounts can access wealth management and investment advice services. These banks also offer loan and mortgage services for clients.

They Have a High Level of Convenience

Traditional banks are more convenient compared to online banks. Despite having online platforms, physical banks still offer in-person customer service. Clients can walk into the banking hall and get the services they need.
Common banks also provide access to ATM services. Clients can withdraw money from these points using their debit cards.

They Offer Both Digital and Traditional Services

Most banks are changing their structure to cope with technological advancements. Clients can now use online services to transact through their accounts. Most banks have a mobile app that allows them to deposit and withdraw money from their accounts.
Clients also can visit the bank and deposit or withdraw cash.

They Accept Cash Deposits

Despite the introduction of paperless cash, most companies still insist on receiving payments in cash. Such companies cannot benefit from fintech companies since they only deal with cash. Traditional banks give them a chance to deposit cash directly to their accounts, making them convenient.

Services Offered by Traditional Banks are Secure

Traditional banks adopt secure systems to protect their clients’ financial details. Common banks have reported very few hacking cases. Most banks are insured; hence clients can still recover their money in case of losses through theft or system hacking.

What are the Disadvantages of Traditional Banking?

Traditional banks are convenient in offering services. However, they also have flaws that make people prefer online banking. Below are some of the major drawbacks associated with traditional banking.

Little or no Interest Rates

Due to the rigid structure and high operational costs, brick-and-mortar banks offer low-interest rates. Some banks also offer interest rates on specific accounts, such as fixed saving accounts. Online banking institutions such as Verv offer high-interest rates.

Traditional Banks have a Wide Range of Fees

Banks are known for their high transaction and service fees. Banks incur high operational costs hence the need to charge customers huge fees. Some of the common fees charged by banks include:

  • Service fees
  • Account maintenance fees
  • Credit and debit card fees
  • Transaction fees

Poor Customer Service

Most well-established banks fail to understand the importance of good customer service. In most cases, clients remain unsatisfied with how some of their issues were solved. Online banks such as Verb thrive in offering exemplary customer service. Customer service provision greatly impacts customer satisfaction; hence, most people prefer online banks vs. traditional banks.

What are the Alternatives to Traditional Banking?

Deciding on the right place to put your money can be very difficult. However, there are several alternatives to choose from apart from the classic banking system. All these alternatives have more advantages compared to common banking systems. Here are some of the alternatives to common banking:

Banking Using Local or Community Banks

Community banks are common in rural areas that mainly depend on farming as the main economic activity. These local banks offer some basic services such as a loan. However, most of the services are more flexible compared to larger banks.

Banking Using Credit Unions

Credit unions are financial institutions owned by members of a certain cooperative. The members run the credit unions voluntarily. Members decide on the interest rates offered on loans making them have better rates on loans. Most of these financial institutions are insured to secure members’ money.

Banking using Online Banks

Online banks are the major alternatives to classic banking. These are banks without any physical location, and clients can only access them through the internet. Online banks offer more affordable services compared to common banks. They are more convenient and accessible at any time.

Choosing Between Traditional Banking vs Modern Banking

Deciding whether to go for online banking vs common banking may be difficult. Due to the very frequent regular banking problems, most people opt for online banks due to their high customer service and convenience. However, not all online banks are the best. It is important to consider safety when choosing an online bank.
