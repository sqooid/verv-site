How to use Business Banking to do Online Business with Ease.

  • Introduction.
  • What is online business banking?
  • What you need to setup an online business bank account.

Introduction.

When you think about banking, there are a few obvious things that come to mind, including long queues, account fees and deposit and withdrawal forms. However, this has long changed with the onset of online payment solutions, especially suited for any online business.

Today, the online financial transaction sector has experienced so much growth, especially over the past ten years that the online business world is rapidly adopting it. As a small online business owner, it is necessary to think about how you are to use business banking to remain profitable. This post will guide you through the entire process of how to use an online business bank.

What is online business banking?

Essentially, business banking online offers multiple services, pretty much like any other conventional banking, including checking accounts. The primary difference is that all the activities for banking online everything is entirely done online.

The only thing you need to do is apply for your banking account online on a bank’s official website and wait for approval. You will then be able to access anything via a mobile app or web browser to start transacting online, especially for cross-border payments.

What you need to setup an online business bank account.

In most cases, you will just need your proof of address, photo ID and credentials to prove your company is registered with the necessary authorities. In some cases, you may come across banks that will help you with the entire registration process to ensure that it is smooth.

However, for some financial services institutions might subject you to credit check to ensure that you’re fit to hold that account.

Reasons why your online business should start using business banking.

As a small online business owner, you stand so much to gain by banking online, especially in terms of saving time and generating value. You can use the save time to do other things that will benefit the growth of your online business. Here are some of the reasons that should guide your decision:

• Seamless syncing with accounting: The element of accounting can be the biggest challenge for any business. When you start using business banking and other accounting systems, you will realize that things will become easier than before because every financial transaction will be synced with the already setup accounting system without having to spend so a lot of time adding any financial transaction manually. Therefore, you financial reports will stay up-to-date.

• You will enjoy improved security: It’s important for any business to manage its books. Therefore, when you start banking online, you can customize employee access to your books. You can filter the number of employees that can access sensitive data and reports. This can be done on a need-to-know basis by providing logins. In case of data breaches, you can easily pin-point the responsible party. It is an ideal to improve security.

• Allows for better organization: Many small businesses overlook this advantage when dealing with business online banking because it is an ideal way to organize your finances. You should think about the convenience that comes with having a business banking account because you will view all your activities and transactions on one dashboard. Therefore, you will easily monitor how your money moves.

• Enjoy worldwide access: With online banking, you don’t have to keep calling your physical bank to get your statement. From any location in the world, you can easily just use your laptop or phone to login into your business account online to verify any online payment, view balances or confirm a cleared check. The fact that it is this convenient should encourage you to start using it.

• Fraud alerts: Since you can easily view your transactions in real time, you can easily trace if something isn’t right and either alert your bank or it will alert you. Furthermore, you can follow up with every transaction to ensure nothing is amiss.

Is business banking safe for online businesses?

In general sense, online banking is just as safe as any brick-and-mortar bank. This is because these digital payment systems have gone through similar scrutiny like any other conventional banks anywhere in the world, especially in the UK and US.

There are some online banks that are already registered with FSCS (Financial Services Compensation Scheme) that ensures that there is protection for funds up to £85,000 for any limited company, charities or small businesses. Therefore, it would be prudent for you to confirm whether the bank you intend to use has signed up to the scheme through the FSCS website.

Nevertheless, before you do anything, you should ensure that the security of any of your devices is up to standard before you proceed to access any of these platforms. Furthermore, it will be up to you to take any required safety measures like avoiding the use of open Wi-Fi networks and ensuring that you use very strong passwords to guard your accounts details.

Conclusion: Is the future of your business online?

Whether you are starting or you own a small online business, you may need to consider using such banking to streamline your financial transactions and processes. This could just be the catalyst you were looking for to elevate your business to the next level. However, this should be done with some careful consideration, including weighing your business needs and wants to help you decide the kind of account that is right for you.
