Blockchain applications in Cloud Banking Technologies

The deployment of blockchain in cloud banking provides untrusted parties with a way to enter into an agreement without relying on the services of a middleman.

By providing a public ledger, cloud banking technologies using blockchain can provide a specific set of financial services, e.g., securitization or payments without requiring a bank.

Additionally, a digital ledger provides its users with additional tools such as self-executing contracts and smart contracts based on a digital ledger.

Application of AI and Cryptographic Ledger to Cloud Banking

Distributed ledger technology and cloud banking technologies have a unique opportunity to disrupt the over $5 trillion banking industry.

It can achieve this by disintermediating the crucial services provided by payment processing companies, including:

1. It Can Facilitate Faster Payments than Banks

Every day, trillions of dollars ripple worldwide through an old-fashioned system of added fees and slow payments presently run by banks.

Banks charge a fee every time they facilitate a cross-border financial transaction, making this a highly profitable sector.
Deploying blockchain in cloud banking will eliminate these fees and reduce the time taken to transmit money from one person to the next.

Additionally, the public transaction ledger provides a cheap and secure way to complete payments, which does away with the need for third-party verifications.

2. Better Clearance and Settlement Systems

A typical bank transfer takes up to three days to complete. The reason for this has to do with how the payments solution and financial infrastructure are built.

The added time is a pain to the client and makes it challenging for banks to move money from one branch to another or from one account to the next.

Currently, a simple payment from one account to another needs to pass through a complicated network of intermediaries before arriving at its final destination.

The distributed ledger technologies can assist in reducing operational costs while bringing its users closer to enjoying real-time financial transactions between banks.

3. Fundraising for Startups During ICOs

Using the existing financial services framework to raise money through venture capital is a laborious process.
An entrepreneur will need to prepare decks and meet with partners requiring them to sit through a series of countless meetings as they negotiate over equity and valuation.

They have to do all this with the hope of getting to exchange a portion of the company for funding. In contrast, some firms are using ICOs powered by a distributed ledger to raise funds.

Initial coin offerings are helping entrepreneurs experiment with a new financing model that unbundles access to money from normal capital raising activities.

4. Blockchain In Cloud Banking Can Help to Create Interoperable Securities

To buy or sell assets such as commodities, debt, and stocks using a digital payment system, you need to have a way of tracking who owns what item.

Financial markets operating today use a complex network of clearinghouses, brokers, central security depositories and exchanges to achieve this fete.

All these parties work using an outdated financial services system that’s slow and prone to errors.
The problem becomes compounded when users attempt to complete a financial transaction using an online payment method.

Applying AI in securities will help tokenize traditional securities such as bonds, stocks, and other alternative assets.
It will place all of them on a public distributed ledger to create more efficiency and allow for the emergence of interoperable capital markets.

5. Using Blockchain in Cloud Banking to Underwrite Loans and Credit Facilities

Traditional banks and payment processors use a credit reporting system to underwrite loans and credit facilities.
Digital ledger technology helps to open up an opportunity for peer-to-peer loans.

Normally, banks require loan applicants to fill a form when applying for a loan. They evaluate each applicant’s creditworthiness by looking at factors such as:

  • Homeownership status
  • Credit score
  • Debt-to-income ratio

They can get this information by requesting a credit report from a credit agency. The use of such a centralized system can prove hostile to consumers.

Adopting cloud banking technologies based on DLT technologies can eliminate the need for gatekeepers in the loans and mortgage industry.

Implementation of a public transaction ledger can assist in making it easier for individuals to borrow money. It can also help reduce the interest rates charged by financial services firms.

6. Increased Transparency and Security in Trade Finance

Trade finance exists to ensure that importers and exporters can engage in global trade, lessen risks, and extend credit.
It plays a crucial role in the global financial system, despite operating on old-fashioned, written documentation instead of an online digital payment system.

Artificial intelligence and cryptographic ledgers present an opportunity to simplify and streamline the complex world of payment processing.

Its implementation can save financiers, importers, and exporters billions of dollars each year.

Furthermore, by replacing the tiresome paper-heavy bills used in the lading process, this technology can create trust and transparency among global trade parties.

7. Fraud Prevention and Customized KYC for Payment Processors

In addition to processing payments and clearing financial transactions, banks also need to onboard new clients, ensure their information is in order, and verify their identity.

The entire process is known as “know your customer” (KYC).

Payment processing companies can spend up to ninety days executing KYC proceedings, causing some patients to become impatient and end their business relationships.

By storing client information on decentralized blocks, DLT in cloud banking can make it easier for banks to share information with payment processors and guarantee its security.
