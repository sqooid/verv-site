Digital Banking Explained: Checking Accounts, Savings Accounts, And More!

Digital banking gives you the ability to access everyday banking tasks via a mobile phone or computer and the capacity to enable cashless transactions at a wide range of establishments. Through banking as a service, you can acquire a digital banking account such as checking accounts, savings accounts, business accounts, and investment accounts.

The phrase “digital banking” involves developing banking services. It also delivers products over electronic means such as ATMs, telephones, the internet, and cellular phones. In the recent past, reports show that investment in digital banking is on the rise.

Due to the rise of digital banking services, traditional banks are enhancing their digital strategies and offering more competitive banking offers.

Below is an extensive review of all digital accounts offered by various banks. It includes features, pros, and cons of these accounts to help the reader make an informed choice.

Digital Banking: All About Checking Accounts

Checking accounts are bank accounts used for daily cash deposits and withdrawals. You can deposit money into a checking account, which later can make payments.

You can deposit your paycheck into your checking account and use it to pay most of your bills every pay cycle. Checking accounts are referred to as “transactional accounts” since they don’t have a restriction on how many transactions you can make or levy fees for excess transactions.

However, don’t just take the account name at face value and think it’s the perfect one for you without doing some research.

The Advantages of Checking Accounts on Digital Banking

  • There’s always a record of your expenditure: Banks track your spending, even if you solely make checks on your account. It could be a carbon copy of a review, an electronic or physical statement from the bank, or check documents already paid out by the bank, making creating an individual budget and managing expenditures a lot easier.
  • Accessibility of your money: A checking account’s goal is to simplify the accessibility of your funds. They use a blend of cheques, debit cards, and internet payment alternatives.

When shopping, all you have to do is swipe your debit card or write a check from your checking account to pay for your purchases. There is no need for cash. If you need to make a cash payment, you can use your card to withdraw money from an ATM.

  • Fees on online checking accounts are lower: Another advantage of online checking accounts is the low fees, which result from lower overhead costs passed on to the customer. Because online banks often do not have specialized ATMs, they usually refund their customers for out-of-network withdrawal fees.

Disadvantages of Checking Accounts

There are a few disadvantages to online checking accounts, including the lack of face-to-face client support. Sitting down with a personal banker at your local branch can help you deal with any account concerns that arise. The best you can hope for is a call centre representative at a toll-free customer service number. Some online banks provide phone customer support 24 hours, while others confine it to typical business hours.

A Review of Savings Accounts

A savings account is a bank account that allows you to keep your money safe while accruing compound interest.

If you need somewhere to keep your money that you’ll utilize to pay bills or cover expenses, a checking account is an ultimate option. On the other hand, a savings account may be a superior option for putting money aside for future needs and objectives.

The Advantages of Saving Accounts

  • Improved monthly savings: Some banks require a minimum balance for sustainability. If the account holder fails to do so, charging fees will apply. There are no monthly maintenance fees, and the interest rate is competitive.
  • You earn interest on savings: The money in your savings account will earn interest from the bank. It’s a fantastic bonus (benefit) though the interest rates are subject to market fluctuations.

Savings accounts allow you to grow your money without putting in any extra effort. Although this isn’t free money (interest earnings must be taxed), it is money you can make passively by saving regularly.

  • Free transfers: You can make unlimited free transfers such as NEFT, RTGS, and card payments with a digital savings account.

The Disadvantages of Saving Accounts

  • Saving accounts have less personalization: If you have an online-only savings account, you won’t be able to make your way to the bank teller to ask a question, make a deposit or get help with your account.
  • It is cumbersome to make deposits: It’s simple to deposit a check into your online savings account by taking a photo of it with your bank’s app. On the other hand, depositing cash requires either finding an ATM that accepts cash deposits or getting a friend cash a check on your behalf.

All About Business Accounts

A business bank account keeps you protected and complying with the law. It also has merits for your clients and workers.

Business accounts allow you to keep track of your cash flows and keep them distinct from your finances. It takes more work to open a company bank account than a personal one.

The Advantages of Business Accounts

  • Accessibility of credit for your business: A line of credit for the corporation in business banking can be used in an emergency or if your company requires new equipment.
  • Professionalism in customer handling: Clients will be able to pay you with checks or credit cards payable to your enterprise rather than directly to you. You’ll also be able to delegate day-to-day banking chores to staff on behalf of the company.

The Disadvantages of Business Accounts

  • The same FDIC insurance limitations apply to business checking accounts to personal accounts.
  • Numerous fees are only applicable to business accounts, not personal checking accounts. A well-established firm, for example, is generally required to maintain a relatively high minimum balance and may be charged fees if it fails to do so.

Everything you Need to know about Investment Accounts

The main goal of an investing account is to earn a higher rate of return than other options, such as a bank savings account. Investment banking is a branch of banking specializing in assisting individuals and businesses in raising funds and providing financial advice.

Banks link security issuers and investors and assist new businesses in going public.

Advantages of Investment Accounts

  • Investment accounts offer the possibility of high investment returns.
  • A personal investment account helps determine how to invest your money in the fund. Investing depends on your risk tolerance and investment criteria. You control your portfolio’s fate.
  • It is possible to make a long-term investment.

Disadvantages of Investment Accounts

  • Returns on a personal investment account are not guaranteed.
  • There is the risk of capital loss.
  • Depending on the type of account you open, long-term investments will ban you from making early withdrawals.

Which is the Best Account for You?

Digital banking is no longer a prospect. It is happening now. New technologies are the driving forces behind digital banking, and If you need a checking account, business account, savings account, or investment account, you can access them digitally. The type of account you need solely depends on the purpose you intend to use it with. Choose one of the many digital accounts and stand to benefit.
